## Category 1: Opening & Rapport

*Purpose: Build connection, put them at ease, get an overview*

### Questions

1. **"Before we dive into the formal questions, is there anything you'd like to know about how today will go?"**
   - *Listen for:* Thoughtfulness, preparation, anxiety level

2. **"Walk me through your background - how did you get to where you are today?"**
   - *Listen for:* Narrative coherence, how they explain transitions, what they emphasize

3. **"What caught your attention about this role?"**
   - *Listen for:* Have they researched you? Generic vs. specific interest

4. **"What do you know about us, and what questions do you have?"**
   - *Listen for:* Preparation level, quality of questions, genuine curiosity

5. **"What's something about you that isn't on your resume?"**
   - *Listen for:* Self-awareness, personality, what they consider noteworthy

### Follow-up Probes
- "Tell me more about that transition..."
- "What made you choose that path?"
- "Looking back, how do you feel about that decision?"

## Category 2: Values & Character

*Purpose: Reveal core values, integrity, what they stand for*

### Questions

6. **"Tell me about a time you had to make a decision that was right but unpopular."**
   - *Listen for:* Moral courage, how they handle disagreement, do they have examples?

7. **"Describe a situation where you saw something wrong at work. What did you do?"**
   - *Listen for:* Do they speak up? How do they handle ethical gray areas?

8. **"What's a professional principle you won't compromise on, even if it costs you?"**
   - *Listen for:* Do they have clear values? Are they self-aware about them?

9. **"Tell me about someone you've worked with who you really respect. What made them stand out?"**
   - *Listen for:* What they value in others reveals what they value in themselves

10. **"When was the last time you changed your mind about something important at work?"**
    - *Listen for:* Intellectual humility, openness to being wrong

11. **"How do you handle a situation where you're asked to do something you disagree with?"**
    - *Listen for:* Respect for hierarchy balanced with speaking up

12. **"Tell me about a time you gave someone difficult feedback. How did you handle it?"**
    - *Listen for:* Direct communication, care for the person, courage

13. **"What would your previous boss say is your biggest weakness? And do you agree?"**
    - *Listen for:* Self-awareness, defensiveness vs. ownership

### Follow-up Probes
- "What made that decision difficult?"
- "How did others react?"
- "Would you do it the same way again?"

## Category 3: Work Style & Culture

*Purpose: Understand how they work, what environment they thrive in*

### Questions

14. **"Describe your ideal work environment. What brings out your best work?"**
    - *Listen for:* Does it match your culture? Self-awareness about needs

15. **"How do you like to receive feedback?"**
    - *Listen for:* Openness to feedback, preferences that match your style

16. **"Tell me about a work culture where you really thrived. What made it great?"**
    - *Listen for:* Cultural preferences, what energizes them

17. **"Tell me about a work culture that wasn't a good fit for you. What didn't work?"**
    - *Listen for:* Self-awareness, potential warning signs for your culture

18. **"How do you manage your time and priorities when everything feels urgent?"**
    - *Listen for:* Organization, prioritization skills, stress management

19. **"Are you more energized by working independently or collaboratively? Give me an example."**
    - *Listen for:* Does their preference match the role?

20. **"How do you handle ambiguity when you're not given clear direction?"**
    - *Listen for:* Comfort with uncertainty, initiative vs. need for structure

21. **"What's your communication style? How do people typically experience working with you?"**
    - *Listen for:* Self-awareness, fit with team dynamics

22. **"How do you recharge when work gets stressful?"**
    - *Listen for:* Self-care awareness, sustainability

23. **"What kind of management style brings out your best work?"**
    - *Listen for:* Does it match how you manage?

### Follow-up Probes
- "Give me a specific example..."
- "What would happen if you were in [opposite situation]?"
- "How would your coworkers describe your style?"

## Category 4: Problem-Solving & Thinking

*Purpose: Assess how they approach challenges, think through problems*

### Questions

24. **"Walk me through the most complex problem you've solved at work. How did you approach it?"**
    - *Listen for:* Structured thinking, creativity, persistence

25. **"Tell me about a time you had to solve a problem with incomplete information."**
    - *Listen for:* Comfort with ambiguity, how they gather info, decision-making

26. **"Describe a time you had to figure something out that no one could teach you."**
    - *Listen for:* Resourcefulness, learning ability, initiative

27. **"How do you approach a problem you've never seen before?"**
    - *Listen for:* Thinking process, use of resources, comfort with unknown

28. **"Tell me about a time your first solution didn't work. What did you do?"**
    - *Listen for:* Persistence, adaptability, how they handle failure

29. **"What's a process or system you improved? What was your approach?"**
    - *Listen for:* Initiative, systems thinking, impact orientation

30. **"How do you decide when something is 'good enough' vs. needs more work?"**
    - *Listen for:* Judgment, perfectionism balance, practical thinking

31. **"Tell me about a time you had to make a quick decision without much time to analyze."**
    - *Listen for:* Decision-making under pressure, gut instincts

32. **"Describe a situation where you had to balance multiple competing priorities."**
    - *Listen for:* Prioritization, judgment, communication about tradeoffs

### Follow-up Probes
- "Walk me through your thought process..."
- "What other options did you consider?"
- "What would you do differently now?"

## Category 5: Teamwork & Collaboration

*Purpose: Understand how they work with others*

### Questions

33. **"Tell me about a time you had to work with someone you found difficult. How did you handle it?"**
    - *Listen for:* Conflict resolution, adaptability, maturity

34. **"Describe a successful team project. What was your role and contribution?"**
    - *Listen for:* Do they share credit? What role do they naturally take?

35. **"Tell me about a time you disagreed with a teammate. How did you resolve it?"**
    - *Listen for:* Conflict style, respect for others, resolution skills

36. **"How do you build trust with new colleagues?"**
    - *Listen for:* Relationship skills, intentionality

37. **"Describe a time you had to give up on your idea because someone else's was better."**
    - *Listen for:* Ego, openness, team orientation

38. **"Tell me about a time you helped a colleague succeed, even when it wasn't your job."**
    - *Listen for:* Generosity, team orientation, initiative

39. **"How do you handle a situation where a teammate isn't pulling their weight?"**
    - *Listen for:* Direct communication, handling vs. complaining

40. **"What role do you typically play in team settings?"**
    - *Listen for:* Self-awareness, flexibility

41. **"Tell me about a time you received feedback from a peer that was hard to hear."**
    - *Listen for:* Openness to feedback, growth response

42. **"How do you keep remote colleagues (or yourself) connected to the team?"**
    - *Listen for:* Proactive communication, relationship building

### Follow-up Probes
- "How did the other person react?"
- "What would you do differently?"
- "What did you learn from that experience?"

## Category 6: Leadership & Initiative

*Purpose: Assess leadership potential and proactive behavior*

### Questions

43. **"Tell me about a time you led something without being asked to."**
    - *Listen for:* Initiative, leadership instinct, results

44. **"Describe a situation where you influenced others without having formal authority."**
    - *Listen for:* Influence skills, persuasion, leadership style

45. **"Tell me about a time you had to motivate a team (or person) through a difficult period."**
    - *Listen for:* Emotional intelligence, leadership under pressure

46. **"What's the most significant change you've driven in a previous role?"**
    - *Listen for:* Change management, impact, initiative

47. **"How do you approach delegating work to others?"**
    - *Listen for:* Trust, development orientation, letting go

48. **"Tell me about a time you had to deliver bad news to your team."**
    - *Listen for:* Communication skills, transparency, care

49. **"Describe how you've developed or mentored someone else."**
    - *Listen for:* Investment in others, patience, teaching ability

50. **"What's your approach when you inherit a problem from someone else?"**
    - *Listen for:* Ownership, blame vs. solutions orientation

51. **"How do you balance getting things done yourself vs. building others' capabilities?"**
    - *Listen for:* Long-term thinking, development focus

### Follow-up Probes
- "What did you learn about leadership from that?"
- "What resistance did you face?"
- "How did you measure success?"

## Category 7: Handling Challenges & Failure

*Purpose: Understand resilience, accountability, and growth from adversity*

### Questions

52. **"Tell me about your biggest professional failure. What happened and what did you learn?"**
    - *Listen for:* Ownership, self-awareness, learning orientation

53. **"Describe a time you were under enormous pressure. How did you handle it?"**
    - *Listen for:* Stress management, coping strategies, resilience

54. **"Tell me about a project that didn't go as planned. What happened?"**
    - *Listen for:* Do they blame others or take ownership?

55. **"What's the most difficult feedback you've ever received? How did you respond?"**
    - *Listen for:* Receptivity, growth from feedback, defensiveness

56. **"Tell me about a time you had to adapt to a major change you didn't agree with."**
    - *Listen for:* Adaptability, attitude toward change, professionalism

57. **"Describe a situation where you felt overwhelmed. What did you do?"**
    - *Listen for:* Self-awareness, coping strategies, asking for help

58. **"Tell me about a time you made a mistake that affected others. How did you handle it?"**
    - *Listen for:* Accountability, communication, making it right

59. **"What's the hardest thing you've had to do professionally?"**
    - *Listen for:* What they consider hard, how they handled it

60. **"How do you bounce back from disappointment?"**
    - *Listen for:* Resilience, coping mechanisms, mindset

### Follow-up Probes
- "What would you do differently?"
- "How long did it take you to recover?"
- "What support did you need?"

64. **"Describe a time you had to learn something quickly to complete a project."**
    - *Listen for:* Learning speed, resourcefulness, adaptability

    - *Listen for:* Proactive learning, industry awareness

    - *Listen for:* Intellectual humility, growth, openness

68. **"How do you approach learning from people who are different from you?"**
    - *Listen for:* Openness, curiosity, diversity of input

## Category 9: Motivation & Career

*Purpose: Understand what drives them and whether your opportunity aligns*

### Questions

70. **"What motivates you to do great work?"**
    - *Listen for:* Intrinsic vs. extrinsic motivation, alignment with role

71. **"What are you looking for in your next role that you don't have now?"**
    - *Listen for:* Can you provide what they're seeking?

72. **"Where do you see yourself in 2-3 years? What would success look like?"**
    - *Listen for:* Does their trajectory match your opportunity?

73. **"What would make you leave a job?"**
    - *Listen for:* Dealbreakers, potential flight risks, values

74. **"Tell me about work that you've found most meaningful. What made it so?"**
    - *Listen for:* Values, what energizes them, passion

75. **"Why are you leaving (or why did you leave) your current/last position?"**
    - *Listen for:* Reasons, any concerning patterns, honesty

76. **"What's something you want to do that you haven't had the chance to do yet?"**
    - *Listen for:* Aspirations, can you offer this?

77. **"If you could design your perfect job, what would it look like?"**
    - *Listen for:* Does it look like this job?

78. **"What matters more to you: the work itself or the people you work with?"**
    - *Listen for:* Priorities, fit with your environment

79. **"Why this company? Why this role? Why now?"**
    - *Listen for:* Genuine interest vs. just need a job

### Follow-up Probes
- "What specifically appeals to you about that?"
- "How would you know you'd achieved that?"
- "What's driving the timing?"

## Category 11: Red Flag Detectors

*Questions designed to surface potential concerns*

### Questions

110. **"Tell me about a time you disagreed with company leadership. What did you do?"**
     - *Red flag:* Excessive complaining, inability to work within systems

111. **"What would your last boss say about working with you - honestly?"**
     - *Red flag:* Can't imagine any criticism, all negative

112. **"Why should we hire you over other candidates?"**
     - *Red flag:* Arrogance, can't articulate value, puts others down

113. **"Tell me about a time you didn't get along with a coworker."**
     - *Red flag:* Never happened (unlikely), always the other person's fault

114. **"What would surprise us about working with you?"**
     - *Red flag:* Warning signs they're revealing, nothing comes to mind

115. **"What's something you're not good at and have decided to accept?"**
     - *Red flag:* Can't acknowledge any weakness, or weakness is core to role

116. **"Tell me about a time you had to work with someone you didn't respect."**
     - *Red flag:* Excessive negativity, inability to find something positive

117. **"How do you handle being told you're wrong?"**
     - *Red flag:* Defensive, can't give an example

118. **"What frustrates you most about work?"**
     - *Red flag:* Things core to this role, long list, inability to manage frustration

119. **"Tell me about a time you took on too much. What happened?"**
     - *Red flag:* Never happened (lack of drive), blamed everyone else

120. **"What's something about your work style that previous managers have had to adjust to?"**
     - *Red flag:* Nothing / denial vs. self-awareness

### Warning Sign Follow-ups
- "Tell me more about that..."
- "How did that situation resolve?"
- "What was your role in that outcome?"

## Category 12: Closing Questions

*Finish strong and gauge their interest*

### Questions

121. **"What questions do you have for me?"**
     - *Listen for:* Quality and thoughtfulness of questions

122. **"Is there anything I haven't asked about that you'd like to share?"**
     - *Listen for:* What they think is important that you missed

123. **"Having learned more about the role, what excites you? What concerns you?"**
     - *Listen for:* Honest assessment, engagement level

124. **"What would you need to see or learn to know this is the right opportunity?"**
     - *Listen for:* Their decision criteria, concerns to address

125. **"How are you feeling about the role at this point?"**
     - *Listen for:* Enthusiasm level, honest concerns

126. **"Is there anything about your experience or skills that you're worried we might not see?"**
     - *Listen for:* Self-advocacy, what they think we might miss

127. **"What timeline are you working with for your decision?"**
     - *Listen for:* Urgency, other opportunities, availability

128. **"Do you have any hesitations about this opportunity?"**
     - *Listen for:* Honest concerns you can address

### Great Candidate Questions to Hope They Ask
- Questions about the team, culture, and people
- Questions about how success is measured
- Questions about challenges and what's hard
- Questions about your leadership style
- Questions about growth and development
- Questions about why YOU are excited about this role

### Concerning Questions (Possible Red Flags)
- Only asking about salary, benefits, time off
- "What's the minimum expected?" questions
- "How quickly can I get promoted?" (without role interest)
- No questions at all

## Quick Reference: Interview Question Selector

### For 30-Minute Screening Interview
Choose 4-5 questions:
- 1 from Opening (#2 or #3)
- 1 from Values (#6 or #8)
- 1 from Problem-Solving (#24 or #26)
- 1 from Motivation (#70 or #79)
- Close with #121

### For 60-Minute Full Interview
Choose 10-12 questions:
- 1-2 from Opening
- 2-3 from Values/Culture
- 2-3 from Role-relevant categories
- 1-2 from Challenges/Failure
- 1-2 from Growth/Motivation
- 1 Red Flag Detector (if concerns exist)
- Close with 2 from Closing

### For Panel Interview (Multiple Interviewers)
Divide categories among interviewers:
- Person A: Opening, Values, Culture
- Person B: Problem-Solving, Role-Specific
- Person C: Leadership, Growth, Motivation
- All: Compare notes on same Closing questions

## Question Quality Checklist

Before using a question, check:

```
□ Is it open-ended? (Not yes/no)
□ Does it ask for a specific example?
□ Is it legal and appropriate?
□ Do I know what a good answer sounds like?
□ Do I know what a red flag sounds like?
□ Is it relevant to this role?
□ Will it differentiate candidates?
```
