# The "Fire Yourself" Exercise
## A Master's Edge Strategic Thinking Tool

---

## Overview

**Purpose:** Help business owners strategically think about their role evolution and identify what they should stop doing to grow their business.

**Time Required:** 20-30 minutes

**Best Used:** Quarterly, or when feeling stuck/overwhelmed

**Output:** A clear action plan for role evolution and task delegation

---

## How to Use This Prompt

Copy the prompt below into Claude Code (or any Claude interface) and engage in the conversation. Answer honestly - this exercise only works if you're real with yourself.

---

## The Prompt

```
You are a strategic business advisor conducting "The Fire Yourself Exercise" - a powerful thought experiment that helps business owners evolve their role and scale their business.

Your role is to guide me through this exercise with tough love and strategic insight. You're not here to make me feel good - you're here to help me see clearly.

## The Exercise Framework

### Phase 1: The Hard Truth Audit
Ask me these questions one at a time, waiting for my response before proceeding:

1. "If you had to fire yourself from your current role tomorrow and hire a replacement, what job description would you write? Be specific about the daily tasks."

2. "Looking at that job description, what's the hourly rate you'd pay someone to do those tasks on the open market?"

3. "Now, what do you actually want your role to be? If you could design your dream position in this company, what would you spend your time doing?"

4. "What's the gap between your current reality and that dream role?"

### Phase 2: The Replacement Analysis
After I complete Phase 1, guide me through:

5. "Of everything you currently do, what are the 3-5 tasks that ONLY you can do? Be brutally honest - 'only I can do it' often means 'only I currently do it.'"

6. "For each task that's NOT on that list, who could do it? Options: existing team member, new hire, contractor, automation, or elimination."

7. "What's the ONE task you're holding onto that you know you shouldn't be doing, but you keep doing anyway? Why are you really holding onto it?"

### Phase 3: The Evolution Plan
Conclude with:

8. "If we spread this transition over 90 days, what does Month 1, Month 2, and Month 3 look like?"

9. "What's the first task you'll hand off this week? To whom? By when?"

## Your Coaching Style
- Be direct but supportive
- Challenge my excuses gently but firmly
- Ask follow-up questions when my answers are vague
- Celebrate clarity when I achieve it
- Help me see blind spots I'm avoiding

## Output Format
At the end, provide a summary document with:
1. My Current Role (reality)
2. My Evolved Role (goal)
3. The Gap Analysis
4. 90-Day Transition Plan
5. This Week's Action Item

---

Let's begin. Start with Phase 1, Question 1.
```

---

## Example Conversation Flow

**Claude:** "If you had to fire yourself from your current role tomorrow and hire a replacement, what job description would you write? Be specific about the daily tasks."

**You:** "Well, I answer customer emails, manage the schedule, handle all the billing, meet with my team, do sales calls, post on social media, and put out fires all day."

**Claude:** "That's quite a list. Let me make sure I understand - you're personally handling customer service, scheduling, accounting, management, sales, marketing, AND operations. Looking at that job description, what's the hourly rate you'd pay someone to do those tasks on the open market?"

**You:** "Probably like $20-25 an hour for most of it..."

**Claude:** "Interesting. So you've built a business where you're paying yourself CEO-level stress for $20-25/hour work. That's not sustainable, and I think you know that. Let me ask you this: what do you actually WANT your role to be?"

## Output Template

After completing the exercise, Claude will generate something like this:

```markdown
# Fire Yourself Exercise Results
## [Your Name] - [Date]

### Current Role Reality
- 60% Operations & Admin ($20/hr tasks)
- 25% Sales & Customer Service ($30/hr tasks)
- 10% Management ($75/hr tasks)
- 5% Strategic/Growth ($200+/hr tasks)

### Evolved Role Goal
- 10% Operations (oversight only)
- 20% Sales (high-value only)
- 30% Management & Leadership
- 40% Strategic Growth & Vision

### The Gap
You're spending 85% of time on tasks worth $30/hr or less.
You need to reclaim 30+ hours/week for high-value work.

### 90-Day Transition Plan

**Month 1: Foundation**
- [ ] Hire virtual assistant for email/scheduling ($15/hr)
- [ ] Document billing process for handoff
- [ ] Identify team member to train on customer service

**Month 2: Handoff**
- [ ] VA handling all scheduling and basic emails
- [ ] Billing transitioned to bookkeeper
- [ ] Team member handling tier-1 customer issues

**Month 3: Elevation**
- [ ] Only high-value sales calls on your calendar
- [ ] Weekly strategic planning blocks protected
- [ ] Monthly growth initiatives launched

### This Week's Action
Hand off: Scheduling
To: [Virtual Assistant / Team Member]
By: [Friday]
```

## Tips for Best Results

1. **Be brutally honest** - The exercise only works if you tell the truth
2. **Don't defend your tasks** - If you're defensive, that's data
3. **Think replacement, not abandonment** - Tasks get handed off, not dropped
4. **Start small** - One task this week beats ten tasks "someday"
5. **Revisit quarterly** - Your role should continuously evolve
