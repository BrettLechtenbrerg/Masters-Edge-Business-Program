## Overview

Your brand book is the DNA of your business. It defines who you are, how you sound, what you stand for, and how you show up in the world. Without it, your marketing is inconsistent, your hiring lacks culture alignment, and your team makes decisions without a North Star.

This tool will guide you through creating a comprehensive brand book covering all 15 essential sections—the same framework used by successful companies to maintain brand consistency across every touchpoint.

## Section-by-Section Guide

### Section 1: Brand Foundation

**What it is:** The core building blocks of your brand identity.

**Questions to answer:**

1. **What is your official business name?** (Include any abbreviations you use)

2. **In one sentence, what do you do?** (Not how—what result do you create?)

3. **Why does your business exist beyond making money?** (Your purpose)

4. **What future are you working toward?** (Your vision—paint a picture of success)

5. **How do you achieve that vision day-to-day?** (Your mission—the work you do)

6. **What makes you different from competitors?** (Your positioning—why choose you?)

**Output Format:**
```
COMPANY NAME: [Name]
ABBREVIATION: [If applicable]

TAGLINE: "[8 words or less]"

BRAND PURPOSE: "[Why you exist]"

VISION STATEMENT: "[The future you're creating]"

MISSION STATEMENT: "[How you achieve the vision]"

POSITIONING STATEMENT: "Unlike [competitors who], [Your Company] [does what differently] for [whom], resulting in [unique value]."
```

---

### Section 2: Brand Values

**What it is:** The non-negotiable principles that guide every decision.

**Questions to answer:**

1. **What would you never compromise on, even if it cost you money?**

2. **What behaviors do you celebrate in your team?**

3. **What would make you fire a client?**

4. **When you're at your best, what values are you embodying?**

5. **What do customers say they love most about working with you?**

**Guidelines:**
- Choose 3-7 core values (more than 7 is too many to remember)
- Each value needs a short description (1 sentence)
- Values should be actionable, not generic
- Avoid clichés like "integrity" unless you can make it specific

**Output Format:**
```
CORE VALUES

1. [Value Name]
   [One-sentence description of what this means in action]

2. [Value Name]
   [One-sentence description]

[Continue for all values]
```

---

### Section 3: Brand Story

**What it is:** Your origin story—the human narrative behind your business.

**Questions to answer:**

1. **What problem did you personally experience that led to starting this business?**

2. **What was the "aha moment" when you realized you had to do something about it?**

3. **What did you try that didn't work? What finally did?**

4. **Who are the key people involved and what do they bring?**

5. **What's the transformation you create for clients?**

6. **What's the bigger mission beyond just your business?**

**Output Format:**
Create two versions:
- **Short Version:** 50-75 words (for bios, social media, quick introductions)
- **Full Version:** 250-400 words (for website About page, press, deep content)

---

### Section 4: Brand Voice & Tone

**What it is:** How your brand sounds in every communication.

**Questions to answer:**

1. **If your brand were a person at a party, how would they talk?**

2. **What 5 adjectives describe how you want to sound?** (e.g., warm, confident, playful)

3. **What words or phrases do you naturally use?** (Your linguistic fingerprint)

4. **What words would you NEVER use?** (Off-brand language)

5. **How does your tone shift in different contexts?** (Social media vs. email vs. crisis)

**Output Format:**
```
TONE OF VOICE SUMMARY
[2-3 paragraph description of your overall voice]

CORE TONE ATTRIBUTES
- [Attribute 1]: [Description] | Example: "[Sample phrase]"
- [Attribute 2]: [Description] | Example: "[Sample phrase]"
[Continue for 4-6 attributes]

TONE BY CONTEXT
- Social Media: [Description]
- Website: [Description]
- Email: [Description]
- Sales Calls: [Description]
- Crisis/Concerns: [Description]

WORDS & PHRASES TO USE
[List of on-brand words grouped by category]

WORDS & PHRASES TO AVOID
[List of off-brand words with reasons why]
```

---

### Section 5: Brand Copy Style

**What it is:** The rules for how you write—formatting, structure, and hierarchy.

**The Transformation Hierarchy:**
Your copy should prioritize impact in this order:

1. **Transformation** (Highest) - Who they become / how their world changes
2. **Outcome** - The result they achieve
3. **Benefit** - What's in it for them
4. **Feature** (Lowest) - What it is/does

**Questions to answer:**

1. **Do you use contractions?** (We'll vs. We will)
2. **First person (we) or third person (The Company)?**
3. **Do you use the Oxford comma?**
4. **What's your headline style?** (Title Case or Sentence case)
5. **Emojis—yes, no, or sometimes?**
6. **How long should paragraphs be?** (Short and punchy or detailed?)

**Output Format:**
```
WRITING PRINCIPLES
1. [Principle]: [Description]
2. [Continue for 5-7 principles]

FORMATTING RULES
- Headlines: [Style]
- Paragraphs: [Length guidelines]
- Bullets: [Style]
- Numbers: [Spelled out vs. numerals]
- Punctuation preferences: [Details]

CAPITALIZATION RULES
- Brand name: [Exact styling]
- Services: [Capitalization rules]
- Industry terms: [Guidelines]

SAMPLE TRANSFORMATION
Before (Generic): "[Feature-led example]"
After (On-Brand): "[Transformation-led rewrite]"
```

### Section 6: Brand Personality

**What it is:** The human characteristics of your brand.

**Questions to answer:**

1. **If your brand were a person, describe them:** (Age, style, energy, interests)

2. **What brand archetype fits best?**
   - The Sage (wisdom, knowledge)
   - The Hero (courage, achievement)
   - The Caregiver (nurturing, service)
   - The Creator (innovation, imagination)
   - The Explorer (freedom, discovery)
   - The Rebel (disruption, revolution)
   - The Lover (passion, connection)
   - The Jester (fun, humor)
   - The Everyman (belonging, authenticity)
   - The Ruler (control, leadership)
   - The Magician (transformation, vision)
   - The Innocent (optimism, simplicity)

3. **What are 5-6 personality traits?** (e.g., warm, witty, bold)

4. **What is your brand definitely NOT?** (Anti-traits)

**Output Format:**
```
BRAND ARCHETYPE
[Archetype Name] – [One-sentence description]

PERSONALITY TRAITS
- [Trait 1]
- [Trait 2]
[Continue for 5-6 traits]

WHAT [BRAND] IS NOT
- [Anti-trait 1]
- [Anti-trait 2]
[Continue]

IF [BRAND] WERE A PERSON
[2-3 sentence vivid description]
```

---

### Section 7: Target Audience

**What it is:** Detailed profiles of who you serve.

**Questions to answer (for each persona):**

1. **Job title and role?**
2. **Company size and industry?**
3. **What keeps them up at night?** (Pain points)
4. **What have they tried that hasn't worked?**
5. **What would success look like for them?**
6. **Where do they hang out online/offline?**
7. **What objections might they have about working with you?**

**Output Format:**
```
PRIMARY AUDIENCES

[Persona Name 1]
- Description: [Brief profile]
- Pain Points: "[In their words]"
- Goals: [What they want]

[Continue for 2-4 personas]

IDEAL CLIENT PROFILE
[Paragraph description of your perfect client]

GEOGRAPHIC FOCUS
- Primary: [Location]
- Secondary: [Location]
```

---

### Section 8: Elevator Pitches

**What it is:** Rehearsed responses to "What do you do?"

**Create three versions:**

1. **15-Second Pitch** (Cocktail party version)
   - One sentence that sparks curiosity
   - Format: "I help [who] [achieve what] so they can [transformation]."

2. **30-Second Pitch** (Networking event version)
   - Expand on the 15-second with how you do it
   - Include what makes you different

3. **Conversational Pitch** (When you have their attention)
   - Start with a relatable problem
   - Position yourself as the solution
   - End with the transformation

---

### Section 9: Key Messages

**What it is:** The core themes that run through all your communications.

**Questions to answer:**

1. **What are the 3-5 main ideas you want people to associate with your brand?**

2. **For each idea, what's the proof or story behind it?**

3. **What messages address the main objections people have?**

**Output Format:**
```
PRIMARY MESSAGING PILLARS

[Pillar 1 Name]
"[Key message statement]"

[Pillar 2 Name]
"[Key message statement]"

[Continue for 3-5 pillars]

SUPPORTING MESSAGES

For [objection/concern]:
"[Response message]"

[Continue for common objections]
```

---

### Section 10: Visual Identity

**What it is:** The visual elements that represent your brand.

**Components to define:**

1. **Primary Colors** (2-4 colors)
   - Color name
   - Hex code
   - Pantone (if known)
   - Usage guidelines

2. **Secondary Colors** (Supporting palette)

3. **Logo Guidelines**
   - Clear space requirements
   - Minimum size
   - Acceptable backgrounds
   - What NOT to do

4. **Image Style**
   - Photography style
   - Illustration style (if applicable)
   - What to avoid

---

### Section 11: Typography

**What it is:** The fonts that represent your brand.

**Components:**
- **Primary Font:** For headlines and key text
- **Secondary Font:** For body copy (if different)
- **Web-safe alternatives:** For email and systems that don't support custom fonts

Include:
- Font name
- Weights used (Regular, Bold, etc.)
- Where to get it (Google Fonts, Adobe, etc.)
- CSS/implementation code

---

### Section 12: Brand Hashtags

**What it is:** Your social media hashtag strategy.

**Categories to create:**
1. **Branded Hashtags** (Your unique tags)
2. **Values/Mission Hashtags** (Your beliefs)
3. **Service-Specific Hashtags** (What you offer)
4. **Industry/Community Hashtags** (Where you belong)
5. **Campaign Hashtags** (For specific initiatives)

**Formula per post:** 1-2 Branded + 2-3 Topical + 2-3 Discovery = 5-8 total

---

### Section 13: Brand Keywords

**What it is:** The search terms and phrases associated with your brand.

**Categories:**
1. **Primary Brand Keywords** (Core terms)
2. **Service Keywords** (What you offer)
3. **Audience Keywords** (Who you serve)
4. **Problem/Solution Keywords** (Pain points you solve)
5. **Long-Tail Keywords** (Specific phrases)
6. **Keywords to Avoid** (Negative keywords for ads)

---

### Section 14: Trademark Guidelines

**What it is:** How to properly use your brand elements.

**Include rules for:**
- Correct spelling and capitalization of brand name
- Logo usage do's and don'ts
- Tagline usage
- Color usage
- Font usage

---

### Section 15: Usage Guidelines

**What it is:** Practical rules for brand consistency.

**Sections:**
- Logo usage rules
- Color application guidelines
- Voice consistency checklist
- Social media guidelines
- Boilerplate copy (50-word and 100-word versions)

---

## Final Compilation Prompt

After completing all sections, use this prompt to compile your brand book:

```
Please compile all the brand elements we've created into a single, professionally formatted Brand Book document with the following structure:

1. Title page with business name and version/date
2. Table of contents
3. All 15 sections with clear headers and formatting
4. Consistent styling throughout
5. Document history section at the end

Format it for easy reference and sharing with team members, contractors, or partners who need to represent the brand.
```
