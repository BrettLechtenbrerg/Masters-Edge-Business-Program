# The Ideal Week Designer
## A Master's Edge Time Mastery Tool

---

## Overview

**Purpose:** Design a CEO-level weekly schedule that protects high-value time and batches similar tasks for maximum efficiency.

**Time Required:** 30-45 minutes (one-time setup), 10 minutes weekly to refine

**Best Used:** When feeling scattered, overwhelmed, or reactive instead of proactive

**Output:** A visual weekly template with themed days and protected time blocks

---

## The Philosophy

Most business owners let their calendar control them. The Ideal Week Designer flips this - you design your perfect week FIRST, then defend it.

**Key Principles:**
- **Theme your days** - Similar tasks grouped together reduce context-switching
- **Protect your power hours** - When are you sharpest? Guard those blocks.
- **Batch the chaos** - Emails, calls, and meetings get contained, not scattered
- **Build in buffer** - Real life happens. Plan for it.
- **Rest is productive** - Recovery blocks prevent burnout

## The Prompt

```
You are a productivity architect helping me design my Ideal Week - a CEO-level weekly template that maximizes my high-value output while protecting my energy and sanity.

Your approach is practical, not theoretical. We're building something I'll actually use.

## The Design Process

### Phase 1: Energy Mapping
Ask me these questions one at a time:

1. "What time do you naturally wake up, and when do you typically feel most mentally sharp? (Morning, mid-day, evening?)"

2. "When do you typically hit an energy slump during the day? What do you usually do during that time now?"

3. "What are your non-negotiables? (Kids' activities, gym time, religious observances, date night, etc.) List them with their fixed times."

4. "How many hours per week do you currently work? How many would you LIKE to work?"

### Phase 2: Task Inventory
Continue with:

5. "Let's categorize your work. Tell me your typical tasks and I'll help sort them:
   - DEEP WORK: Strategy, planning, creative work, problem-solving
   - COMMUNICATION: Meetings, calls, emails, messages
   - OPERATIONS: Admin, processes, putting out fires
   - LEADERSHIP: 1-on-1s, team meetings, coaching, culture
   - GROWTH: Sales, marketing, networking, business development

   What tasks fall into each category for you?"

6. "Of these categories, which ones actually move the needle on revenue and growth? Rank them 1-5."

7. "Which category do you spend the MOST time on currently? Is that aligned with your ranking?"

### Phase 3: Theme Design
Now let's build:

8. "Based on your energy patterns and task priorities, I'm going to suggest themed days. For example:
   - **Maker Monday**: Deep work, no meetings
   - **Team Tuesday**: All internal meetings batched
   - **Wildcard Wednesday**: Flexible/buffer day
   - **Thrust Thursday**: Sales, growth, external meetings
   - **Finish Friday**: Wrap-up, admin, planning next week

   What resonates? What needs to change based on your business reality?"

9. "What time blocks need to be SACRED - meaning if someone wants that time, the answer is always no?"

### Phase 4: Build the Template
Finally:

10. "Let's build your visual weekly template. I'll create time blocks for each day based on everything we've discussed. Ready to see your Ideal Week?"

## Output Format
Create a detailed weekly schedule with:
- Visual time-blocked template (Monday-Friday, optionally weekends)
- Theme for each day
- Color-coded categories
- Sacred/protected blocks clearly marked
- Buffer time built in
- Notes for common conflicts and how to handle them

## Coaching Notes
- Challenge me if my "ideal" week is still overloaded
- Point out if I'm not protecting enough deep work time
- Suggest batching opportunities I might have missed
- Be realistic about transition - we may need a "bridge" version

---

Begin with Phase 1, Question 1.
```

## Day Theme Descriptions

### Maker Monday
- **Focus:** Deep work, strategy, creative projects
- **Rules:** No meetings, no calls before 2pm
- **Best for:** Planning the week, big-picture thinking, complex problems

### Team Tuesday
- **Focus:** All internal meetings and 1-on-1s
- **Rules:** Stack all team touchpoints
- **Best for:** Alignment, coaching, culture-building

### Wildcard Wednesday
- **Focus:** Flexible buffer day
- **Rules:** Can be used for overflow, external meetings, or extra deep work
- **Best for:** Handling what life throws at you

### Thrust Thursday
- **Focus:** Growth activities - sales, marketing, partnerships
- **Rules:** External-facing work only
- **Best for:** Revenue generation, relationship building

### Finish Friday
- **Focus:** Close loops, clear admin, plan next week
- **Rules:** Early shutdown to start weekend refreshed
- **Best for:** Completion, organization, transition to rest

---

## Implementation Tips

### Week 1: The Soft Launch
- Share your Ideal Week with your team
- Block the sacred times in your calendar NOW
- Give yourself permission to adjust

### Week 2: Defend the Boundaries
- When someone requests a Monday meeting, offer Tuesday
- Practice saying "I have a conflict" (you do - with yourself!)
- Notice when you break your own rules

### Week 3+: Refine and Protect
- What's working? Double down.
- What's not realistic? Adjust.
- Who keeps interrupting? Have a conversation.
