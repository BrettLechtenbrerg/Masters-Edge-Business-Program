## Template 2: Pricing Change Response Matrix

When a competitor changes pricing, use this decision framework:

### They RAISED Prices

| Scenario | Your Response Options |
|----------|----------------------|
| **They raised prices, you're now cheaper** | 1. Hold your price (capture value-seekers) <br> 2. Raise yours too (industry is moving up) <br> 3. Emphasize your value (same quality, better price) |
| **They added premium tier** | 1. Consider your own premium tier <br> 2. Position as "all-inclusive" alternative <br> 3. Target the customers priced out |
| **They raised only certain tiers** | 1. Analyze which tiers — why those? <br> 2. Adjust your comparable tiers <br> 3. Target the displaced segment |

### They LOWERED Prices

| Scenario | Your Response Options |
|----------|----------------------|
| **They dropped prices significantly** | 1. DON'T panic match <br> 2. Emphasize quality/service difference <br> 3. Offer value-adds vs. cutting price <br> 4. Target customers who value quality over price |
| **They introduced a "free" or "cheap" tier** | 1. Consider a limited free option <br> 2. Emphasize what free doesn't include <br> 3. Create compelling upgrade path from their free to your paid |
| **They're running a promo/discount** | 1. Wait it out (temporary) <br> 2. Counter with your own (if needed) <br> 3. Use the noise to highlight your stability |

## Template 5: Competitive Response Playbook

Pre-planned responses for common competitive scenarios.

### Scenario: Competitor Launches New Feature We Don't Have

```
TRIGGER: New feature detected on competitor services page

IMMEDIATE (Within 24 hours):
□ Assess: Do our customers need this?
□ Check: Are we hearing about this from sales/support?
□ Evaluate: How hard would this be for us to build/buy?

IF customers are asking for it:
□ Add to product roadmap for evaluation
□ Create "coming soon" messaging if appropriate
□ Train sales on positioning against this feature

IF customers aren't asking:
□ Monitor for 30 days
□ Track if it comes up in competitive deals
□ Consider if this indicates a market shift

NEVER DO:
□ Panic and announce a feature you can't deliver
□ Discount to compensate for missing feature
□ Badmouth the competitor's new feature
```

### Scenario: Competitor Runs Aggressive Discount Campaign

```
TRIGGER: Major discount detected on competitor pricing page

IMMEDIATE (Within 24 hours):
□ Determine: Temporary promo or permanent change?
□ Assess: What's their likely motivation? (slow sales? new investor pressure?)
□ Alert: Notify sales team about competitive landscape

IF temporary promotion:
□ DO NOT match with your own discount
□ Prepare sales with talking points on value
□ Consider counter-offer for at-risk deals only
□ Wait it out — desperation discounting rarely works

IF permanent price drop:
□ Evaluate your pricing (are we overpriced?)
□ Emphasize value and service difference
□ Consider adding value vs. cutting price
□ Target their existing customers who may feel devalued

NEVER DO:
□ Match their discount automatically
□ Start a price war you can't win
□ Assume their customers will flood to them
```

### Scenario: Competitor Hires Key Executive

```
TRIGGER: New leadership detected on competitor about/team page

IMMEDIATE (Within 48 hours):
□ Research: Who is this person? Background?
□ Assess: What does this hire signal about their strategy?
□ Predict: What changes might this person drive?

IF sales/GTM leader:
□ Expect increased competitive activity in 60-90 days
□ Shore up customer relationships proactively
□ Prepare sales team for potential new tactics

IF product/tech leader:
□ Expect product changes in 6-12 months
□ Monitor their job postings for clues
□ Consider if we should accelerate our roadmap

IF marketing leader:
□ Expect messaging/brand changes
□ Watch for new campaigns and content
□ Prepare to counter new positioning

ALWAYS DO:
□ Document this in competitor file
□ Set calendar reminder to review in 90 days
□ Brief relevant team members
```

## Template 6: Competitive Intelligence Database

Track all competitors in one place. Create in Google Sheets, Notion, or GHL Custom Object.

### Fields to Track:

| Field | Type | Purpose |
|-------|------|---------|
| **Company Name** | Text | Competitor name |
| **Website** | URL | Main website |
| **Category** | Dropdown | Direct / Indirect / Adjacent |
| **Priority** | Dropdown | Primary / Secondary / Watch |
| **Founded** | Date | Company age |
| **Est. Revenue** | Currency | Size indicator |
| **Est. Employees** | Number | Size indicator |
| **Funding** | Currency | Resources available |
| **Pricing (Low)** | Currency | Entry price point |
| **Pricing (High)** | Currency | Top price point |
| **Key Differentiator** | Text | Their main selling point |
| **Our Differentiator** | Text | How we beat them |
| **Target Customer** | Text | Who they serve |
| **Strengths** | Long Text | What they do well |
| **Weaknesses** | Long Text | Where they're vulnerable |
| **Recent Changes** | Long Text | Last 30 days of intel |
| **Last Reviewed** | Date | When we last analyzed |
| **Notes** | Long Text | Additional observations |

### Sample Entry:

```
Company Name: Acme Corp
Website: acme.com
Category: Direct
Priority: Primary
Founded: 2018
Est. Revenue: $5M
Est. Employees: 40
Funding: $2M (Seed)
Pricing (Low): $29/mo
Pricing (High): $199/mo
Key Differentiator: "AI-powered automation"
Our Differentiator: Personal service + simpler UI
Target Customer: SMBs 10-50 employees
Strengths: Good marketing, strong SEO, nice UI
Weaknesses: Poor support, complex onboarding, feature bloat
Recent Changes: Raised prices 15% on Jan 15. Added AI features.

Notes: Seems to be moving upmarket. Watch for enterprise push.
```

## Using Intel in Daily Operations

### In Sales Calls

> "I noticed [Competitor] just raised their prices. How does that affect your evaluation?"

> "Many of our customers switched from [Competitor] because of [specific weakness]. Is that something you've experienced?"

### In Marketing

- Create comparison content: "[Us] vs [Competitor]: What's the difference?"
- Target competitor keywords in SEO/PPC
- Develop case studies from customers who switched
- Address competitor claims in your messaging

### In Product Development

- Track features competitors are adding
- Identify gaps competitors are filling that you aren't
- Prioritize based on competitive necessity vs. innovation
- Learn from competitor UX and design

### In Strategic Planning

- Annual competitive landscape review
- Identify emerging competitors early
- Predict market shifts from competitor patterns
- Inform pricing and positioning decisions

## Converting Intel to Action

The gap between "interesting" and "useful" is action. For every piece of intel, ask:

1. **So what?** — What does this mean for our business?
2. **Now what?** — What should we do about it?
3. **Who owns it?** — Who's responsible for the action?
4. **By when?** — What's the deadline?
5. **How will we measure?** — How do we know if it worked?

If you can't answer these questions, the intel is just trivia.
