# The Vendor Negotiation Prep
## A Master's Edge Money-Saving Tool

---

## Overview

**Purpose:** Prepare for any vendor conversation (new contract, renewal, dispute, or renegotiation) with a strategic game plan that saves money and protects your interests.

**Time Required:** 15-20 minutes

**Best Used:** Before ANY vendor call where money, terms, or commitments are being discussed

**Output:** A complete negotiation prep sheet with talking points, alternatives, and walk-away criteria

---

## The Philosophy

**Most business owners leave money on the table** because they:
- Don't prepare before calls
- Don't know their alternatives (BATNA)
- Feel awkward negotiating
- Accept the first offer
- Don't know what's negotiable

**The Vendor Negotiation Prep changes this by:**
- Forcing you to know your position BEFORE the call
- Identifying leverage you didn't know you had
- Giving you specific phrases to use
- Defining your walk-away point in advance

---

## How to Use This Prompt

Copy the prompt below into Claude Code before any vendor conversation. Complete the prep, then have the conversation. You'll be amazed how different it feels when you're prepared.

---

## The Prompt

```
You are a negotiation strategist helping me prepare for an upcoming vendor conversation. Your job is to help me understand my position, identify my leverage, and prepare specific talking points so I walk into this conversation confident and ready.

## The Negotiation Prep Framework

### Phase 1: Know the Situation
Ask me these questions one at a time:

1. "What vendor are you negotiating with, and what product/service is this about?"

2. "What's the context? (New purchase, renewal, renegotiation, dispute, cancellation attempt, etc.)"

3. "What are you currently paying (or being quoted), and what do you WANT to pay or achieve?"

4. "When does the current contract end, or when do you need to make a decision by?"

5. "How important is this vendor to your business? (Critical, important, nice-to-have, or considering leaving)"

### Phase 2: Know Your Position

6. "What alternatives do you have? List competitors or workarounds you could use instead."

7. "What's your BATNA (Best Alternative To Negotiated Agreement)? If this negotiation fails completely, what's your Plan B?"

8. "What leverage do you have? Think about:
   - How long you've been a customer
   - How much you spend with them
   - Whether you've referred others
   - Your growth potential
   - Your influence/reach
   - Industry relationships"

9. "What do they want from you? (Multi-year commitment, case study, referral, upsell, etc.)"

### Phase 3: Know What's Negotiable

10. "Beyond price, what else could be negotiated? Consider:
    - Payment terms (net 30 vs net 60)
    - Contract length
    - Service levels / SLAs
    - Additional features or seats
    - Training or support
    - Exit clauses
    - Price locks for future years"

### Phase 4: Prepare the Conversation

11. "What's your opening position? (Always start more aggressive than your target)"

12. "What's your target position? (What you'd be happy with)"

13. "What's your walk-away point? (Below this, you say no or leave)"

14. "What objections do you expect from them, and how will you respond?"

## Output Format
Generate a complete Negotiation Prep Sheet including:
1. Situation Summary
2. My Position vs Their Position
3. My Leverage Points
4. BATNA (walk-away alternative)
5. What's Negotiable Beyond Price
6. Opening → Target → Walk-Away Points
7. Key Talking Points (specific phrases to use)
8. Objection Responses
9. Questions to Ask Them
10. Post-Call Checklist

## Coaching Notes
- Help me identify leverage I might not see
- Challenge me if my expectations are unrealistic
- Give me SPECIFIC phrases to use, not just concepts
- Remind me that silence is a negotiation tool
- Help me stay firm without being aggressive

---

Let's begin. What vendor are you negotiating with?
```

## Example Negotiation Prep Sheet

```markdown
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
              VENDOR NEGOTIATION PREP SHEET
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

VENDOR: SaaS Company XYZ
PRODUCT: Project Management Software
CONTEXT: Annual renewal coming up, want to reduce cost
DATE OF CALL: January 28, 2025
CURRENT SPEND: $500/month ($6,000/year)
TARGET OUTCOME: $350/month or equivalent value add

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SITUATION ANALYSIS
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

MY POSITION:
• 3-year customer, always paid on time
• Using 15 of 25 seats (paying for unused capacity)
• Haven't used 2 major features we're paying for
• Referred 2 other businesses to them
• Contract ends Feb 15 - they need us to renew

THEIR LIKELY POSITION:
• Want to keep us as a customer (retention metric)
• Probably have renewal targets this quarter
• Would prefer multi-year commitment
• May try to upsell additional features

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
MY LEVERAGE POINTS
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

✓ Loyal 3-year customer (low churn risk for them)
✓ Paid on time every month (reliable revenue)
✓ Referred 2 customers (worth ~$12K ARR to them)
✓ Not using all features (can downgrade or leave)
✓ Competitors are actively pursuing us
✓ Their retention team has Q1 targets to hit

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
MY BATNA (Best Alternative)
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

If this negotiation fails, I will:
• Switch to Competitor B (quoted $300/month for equivalent)
• Migration would take ~2 weeks
• Some training needed, but manageable
• Already have demo scheduled as backup

BATNA STRENGTH: 7/10 (Strong - I have a real alternative)

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
WHAT'S NEGOTIABLE (Beyond Price)
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

□ Price reduction (primary goal)
□ Reduce to 15 seats (match actual usage)
□ Remove unused features / downgrade plan
□ Add additional training sessions
□ Extended payment terms (Net 60 vs Net 30)
□ Price lock for 2 years
□ Better SLA / dedicated support
□ Exit clause improvements (30 days vs 90)

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
NEGOTIATION POSITIONS
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

                    OPENING    TARGET    WALK-AWAY
Monthly price:       $300       $350      $425
Or equivalent:       40% off    30% off   15% off
Contract length:     1 year     1 year    2 year max
Seats:              15         15        20

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
KEY TALKING POINTS (Use These Exact Phrases)
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

OPENING:
"Thanks for jumping on this call. I've been a happy customer for
three years, but I'm doing a review of all our software costs,
and I need to make sure the numbers work for us to continue."

ESTABLISHING LEVERAGE:
"I've referred two other businesses to you, we've never missed
a payment, and we've been loyal customers. I'm hoping that
loyalty is worth something as we discuss renewal."

NAMING THE GAP:
"Looking at our usage, we're paying for 25 seats but using 15,
and there are two features we've never touched. I want to make
sure I'm paying for what I actually use."

INTRODUCING COMPETITION (if needed):
"I'll be honest - I've had a demo with [Competitor], and their
pricing is significantly lower for what we need. I'd prefer to
stay with you, but the numbers need to make sense."

ASKING FOR DISCOUNT:
"What can you do on price to keep us as a customer?"
[Then STAY SILENT. Let them respond first.]

RESPONDING TO FIRST OFFER:
"I appreciate that, but that's not quite where I need to be.
What else can you do?"

IF THEY SAY NO:
"I understand you have constraints. Let me think about this
and explore my options. When is the last day I can decide?"

CLOSING (if successful):
"If you can get me [X], I can commit to renewing today.
Can you make that happen?"

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
OBJECTION RESPONSES
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

"That's our best price":
→ "I understand list pricing has limits, but I'm a 3-year customer
   who's referred business to you. There must be something you can
   do for loyalty. Can you check with your manager?"

"We can't do monthly discounts, only annual prepay":
→ "If I prepay annually, what discount does that unlock? And can
   we add a 30-day exit clause if things change?"

"That price is only for new customers":
→ "So you're telling me new customers get better treatment than
   loyal ones? That doesn't seem right. How do we fix that?"

"I'll need to check with my manager":
→ "Of course. What do you think you can get approved? I want to
   give you ammunition to advocate for us."

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
QUESTIONS TO ASK THEM
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

• "What flexibility do you have on pricing for existing customers?"
• "Is there a downgrade option that better matches our usage?"
• "What would it take to get a price lock for multiple years?"
• "Are there any promotions or loyalty discounts available?"
• "If I commit to a 2-year term, what additional value can you add?"
• "What's the best deal you've given a customer in my situation?"

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
POST-CALL CHECKLIST
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

After the call:
□ Document exactly what was offered
□ Get any verbal agreements in writing/email
□ Review before signing anything
□ If unsatisfied, ask to escalate to manager
□ Don't feel pressured to decide immediately
□ Compare final offer to BATNA

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
```

## Universal Negotiation Phrases

Keep these in your back pocket for any vendor conversation:

### Opening Strong
- *"I need to make sure the numbers work for us to continue."*
- *"I'm reviewing all our vendor relationships right now."*
- *"I want to stay with you, but I need you to meet me halfway."*

### Creating Leverage
- *"I've been a loyal customer for X years."*
- *"I've referred [#] customers to you."*
- *"I've got competitive quotes I'm considering."*
- *"My contract is up - I'm evaluating all options."*

### Asking for More
- *"What can you do on price?"* (Then stay silent)
- *"That's not quite where I need to be. What else can you do?"*
- *"If I commit today, what can you add?"*
- *"Help me justify this to my [partner/team/board]."*

### When They Push Back
- *"I understand. Let me think about it and explore my options."*
- *"Can you check with your manager?"*
- *"What would you do if you were in my position?"*
- *"I'm not looking to squeeze you - I'm looking for fair."*

### Closing
- *"If you can get me X, I can commit today."*
- *"Send me that in writing and we have a deal."*
- *"I need [specific thing] to make this work."*

## Quick Prep for Surprise Calls

If a vendor calls unexpectedly and wants to discuss renewal/pricing:

> *"Thanks for calling. I'm in the middle of something right now, but I'd love to discuss this properly. Can we schedule a call for [tomorrow/next week]?"*

**Never negotiate unprepared.** Take the time to use this framework first.
