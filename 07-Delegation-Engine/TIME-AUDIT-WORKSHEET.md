# Time Audit Worksheet
## Part of The Delegation Engine

---

## Overview

**Purpose:** Track where your time actually goes so you can make data-driven decisions about what to delegate.

**Time Required:** 5-10 minutes daily for one week, OR 30 minutes for an estimate-based audit

**Why It Matters:** Most business owners dramatically underestimate time spent on low-value tasks and overestimate time on high-value work. This worksheet reveals the truth.

---

## Two Ways to Complete This Audit

### Option A: Real-Time Tracking (Most Accurate)
Track your time for one full work week. Every time you switch tasks, note:
- What you did
- How long it took
- What category it falls into

**Best for:** Owners who suspect they're "lying to themselves" about time use

### Option B: Estimate-Based Audit (Faster)
Use the prompt below to estimate your typical week from memory.

**Best for:** Getting started quickly, or when real-time tracking isn't practical

## Option B: Estimate-Based Audit Prompt

Copy this prompt into Claude Code for a guided estimate-based audit:

```
You are a Time Audit Coach helping me estimate how I spend my work week. Your job is to help me build an accurate picture of where my time goes so I can identify delegation opportunities.

Be thorough - help me remember time I might forget or underestimate.

## The Time Audit Process

### Part 1: The Big Picture

1. "How many hours do you typically work per week? (Include all work - evenings, weekends, etc.)"

2. "Walk me through a TYPICAL day. Start from when you begin working until you stop. What does a Monday look like? Just describe it naturally."

3. "Are other days similar, or do you have different patterns on different days? Tell me about any variations."

### Part 2: Category Deep-Dive

Now let's get specific. I'll ask about each category:

4. **ADMIN & OPERATIONS**
   "How much time per week do you spend on:
   - Email (reading, responding, organizing)?
   - Scheduling (your calendar, appointments, meetings)?
   - Paperwork and documentation?
   - General admin tasks?"

5. **CUSTOMER SERVICE**
   "How much time per week do you spend on:
   - Responding to customer questions?
   - Handling complaints or issues?
   - Customer communication (non-sales)?
   - Support activities?"

6. **FINANCIAL**
   "How much time per week do you spend on:
   - Invoicing and billing?
   - Bookkeeping or accounting tasks?
   - Paying bills and managing cash flow?
   - Financial planning or review?"

7. **SALES**
   "How much time per week do you spend on:
   - Sales calls and meetings?
   - Writing proposals or quotes?
   - Follow-ups with prospects?
   - Networking and relationship building?"

8. **MARKETING**
   "How much time per week do you spend on:
   - Content creation (posts, videos, blogs)?
   - Social media management?
   - Advertising and campaigns?
   - Marketing planning and strategy?"

9. **FULFILLMENT**
   "How much time per week do you spend on:
   - Actually delivering your product/service?
   - Production or creation?
   - Quality control?
   - Project execution?"

10. **MANAGEMENT**
    "How much time per week do you spend on:
    - Team meetings?
    - 1-on-1s with team members?
    - Hiring, training, performance management?
    - Managing vendors or contractors?"

11. **STRATEGY**
    "How much time per week do you spend on:
    - Business planning and strategy?
    - Major decisions?
    - Analysis and research?
    - Vision and direction work?"

12. **INTERRUPTIONS**
    "How much time per week do you lose to:
    - Unexpected fires and emergencies?
    - Unplanned requests from others?
    - Context switching between tasks?
    - Things that just 'come up'?"

### Part 3: Reality Check

13. "Let's add up the totals. Does this number match the hours you said you work? If not, where's the gap?"

14. "Looking at this breakdown, what surprises you? What's higher or lower than you expected?"

15. "Which category do you WISH you spent more time on? Which do you wish you spent less time on?"

## Output Format

Generate a Time Audit Report including:

1. **Weekly Time Breakdown**
   - Hours per category
   - Percentage of total time
   - Visual chart (ASCII or description)

2. **Reality vs. Aspiration**
   - Where time actually goes
   - Where you want time to go
   - The gap

3. **Initial Delegation Candidates**
   - Tasks taking significant time
   - Tasks in low-value categories
   - Tasks you expressed frustration about

4. **Key Insights**
   - Patterns identified
   - Surprising findings
   - Opportunities spotted

---

Let's begin. How many hours do you typically work per week?
```

## Tips for Accurate Tracking

### Do:
- Track in real-time (don't try to remember later)
- Include EVERYTHING (even 5-minute tasks)
- Be honest about interruptions and distractions
- Note when you work evenings/weekends

### Don't:
- Round to make yourself look better
- Skip the "embarrassing" time uses
- Forget transition time between tasks
- Ignore time on your phone/email outside "work hours"
